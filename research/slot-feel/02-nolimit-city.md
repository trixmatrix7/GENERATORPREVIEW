# Nolimit City — the formula

> Research sweep 2026-07-16. Estimates are marked as estimates.

## Executive summary

Nolimit City's "feel" is a tightly coupled system: interlocking proprietary mechanics (xWays, xNudge, xSplit, xBomb, Enhancer Cells) that all feed one visible win-multiplier economy; extreme, honestly-published volatility (10/10 ratings, "1 in N spins" stats, max-win odds printed in the game info); dark cinematic art with diegetic, theme-authentic audio built in Ableton by an in-house composer who reverbs "99% of all sound effects" to place them in the game's physical space; and a fast, grinder-friendly base loop (Action Spins run multiple rounds per second). Win presentation escalates from modest line pays to record-culture max-win ceremonies — Nolimit even ships framed "Max Win Legend" posters to the first five players who hit a game's max win. The formula: every mechanic is a multiplier delivery vehicle, every sound is diegetic to the theme, every stat is disclosed, and the max win is a named, marketed destination.

## Studio identity and the signature six

Nolimit City (Stockholm/Malta, co-founded by Jonas Tegman; acquired by Evolution 2022) deliberately targets "a new generation of gamers who frequent Twitch and YouTube" with high-risk/high-reward math and themes "no one else dares to touch" — asylums, prisons, mining disasters, execution. Product owner Per Lindheimer: generic themes are "boring"; they make "slot games for an adult audience," and on controversial themes: "It's what we wanna do. And also what's been very well received by the players." They run three portfolios: **Labs** (new mechanics), **classic Nolimit City**, and **Over the Limit** (most experimental).

**The signature games and what each contributes:**
- **Fire in the Hole (xBomb, 2021)** — 6x3→6x6 collapsing mine, 486→46,656 ways, 60,000x max, 96.06% RTP, hit freq ~1 in 4, bonus ~1 in 200. "Heavy plodding soundtrack… glowing lanterns illuminate an old mine shaft propped up by timber support beams." The template for cascade+multiplier NLC games.
- **Tombstone R.I.P (2021)** — the volatility flagship: 300,000x max win, volatility branded "Insane" (10/10+), 108 lines. xNudge wilds only on middle 3 reels, multipliers SUM when multiple land. Hang 'em High bonus ~1 in 194 spins, multipliers never reset; Boothill scatter upgrade (+2 spins). Real record hit: 271,754x (Bigwinboard-documented).
- **Mental (2021)** — asylum horror. 5 reels in 3-2-3-2-3 layout, 108 ways, expandable to 20 rows via Fire Frames (1–13 flaming positions split symbols). Dead Patients reveal multipliers x5 up to x9,999. 66,666x max; max-win odds ~1 in 18M spins (published). Three FS tiers: Autopsy (8), Lobotomy (9), Mental Spins (10).
- **San Quentin xWays (2021)** — prison, "cartoon, humorous meme-like approach" softening the grimness (inmates named Beefy Dick, Loco Luis…). 150,000x max, hit freq 34.25%, bonus 1 in 230. Jumping Wilds whose multipliers double on Razor Splits up to x512. Reviewers logged ~500 spins without exceeding 15x in base — that drought is intentional.
- **Dead Canary (2022)** — mine-grotto, 6 reels starting 3-3-2-2-1-1 expanding to 3-4-3-4-3-4, cascade removals; bonus buys 68x / 500x.
- **Folsom Prison (2022)** — Johnny Cash-adjacent: "bluesy rock sound that leans towards punk in free spins." Grid starts at only 16 ways (2 active positions on reels 1–4) and unlocks to 4-6-6-6-4 = 46,664 ways; 75,000x triggers the 'Meet Your Maker' sequence. The locked-grid-you-must-open loop is a core NLC pattern (also Fire in the Hole, Mental).

## The xMechanics: how each works and how it FEELS

All mechanics are multiplier/ways delivery vehicles that visibly transform the grid — the player watches potential grow before the win is evaluated. That sequencing (transform → then pay) is the core of the feel.

- **xWays** — mystery symbol reveals a stack of 2–4 of one paying symbol, growing that reel's height and multiplying ways. Feel: a flip/burst reveal, cells physically expand the reel (San Quentin, Punk Rocker, xWays Hoarder). *Estimate: reveal is ~200–300ms per xWays with a bright 'pop' sting; multiple xWays reveal sequentially, not simultaneously, to stack anticipation.*
- **xNudge** — stacked wild lands partially off-grid, then nudges step-by-step until fully visible; **each nudge step = +1 multiplier**. Multiple xNudge wild multipliers ADD together (Tombstone, Deadwood, Dragon Tribe). Lindheimer's favorite: "Potent, fun — always makes my heart run when I hit it." Feel: each nudge is a discrete mechanical 'thunk' with the multiplier badge ticking up in sync — the payout literally ratchets upward in front of you. *Estimate: ~150–250ms per nudge step, one bass-heavy transient per step, often a short riser under the sequence; the multiplier number punches/scales on each tick.*
- **xSplit** — a razor/blade symbol splits symbols (in xWays Hoarder: all symbols to its left) into two, doubling positions, then becomes wild. Tombstone RIP's reel-5 xSplit Wild "slices across the grid to split all symbols to their left in half." Feel: a slash-flash sweep across the grid with a knife 'shing' + wet split; instantly doubles visible density. *Estimate: sweep travels right-to-left in ~300–500ms.*
- **xBomb** — wild that explodes on ANY win (even uninvolved), destroying adjacent symbols (not scatters) and adding **+1 to the persistent cascade multiplier**; explosion also triggers a collapse that can unlock a new row (Fire in the Hole). Feel: fuse-hiss → detonation → debris → grid physically grows taller. The +1 multiplier lands exactly on the detonation frame.
- **Enhancer Cells** — locked cell positions above/below the reels (San Quentin) or beside reels 2/4 (Mental, unlocked at 4 or 6 Fire Frames) that randomly crack open to reveal wilds, xWays, Razor Splits, or high pays. Feel: dormant real estate that visibly "wants" to open — a per-spin lottery layered on top of the reels.
- **Supporting cast:** Jumping Wilds (persist and teleport each free spin, multipliers doubling on Razor Splits to x512 — San Quentin), Infectious xWays (revealed symbol infects all matching visible symbols into stacks — East Coast vs West Coast), xPays (cascade variant, Monkey's Gold), Wild Mining (3–5 matching symbols in a dead horizontal line convert to 1–3 wilds and blow the board — Fire in the Hole; brilliant: it converts NEAR-MISSES into events), and the punitive **xRIP** (Tombstone RIP: total spin wins below your stake are voided — volatility as a feature).

**Key design lesson:** the mechanics interlock — xBomb feeds the collapse which unlocks rows which raises ways which xWays multiplies. Nothing is a standalone gimmick; everything compounds into one number the player is watching.

## Win presentation and max-win culture

- **Base game is dry on purpose.** San Quentin review: nearly 500 spins before exceeding 15x. Small wins get minimal fanfare — line highlight, terse sound cue. Reviewers note "audio hints are acute, harsh, and sometimes reduced to the minimum." The contrast IS the presentation: base = starvation, bonus = feast.
- **The multiplier chain is the show, not the counter.** Big wins are presented as a visible causal chain: nudge ticks, bomb detonations, split sweeps, each incrementing an on-screen multiplier badge, THEN the total resolves. Reviewers: NLC's "cinematic presentation… turns even a simple spin into a dramatic sequence of suspense and potential."
- **Big-win screens are themed set pieces**, praised for "terrifyingly accurate ideas poured into symbol design, big win screens, and feature names." Mental's signature move: the big-win **count-up finishes in eerie silence** — the celebration cuts to nothing, matching the asylum dread. That subversion (silence as the payoff punctuation) is a trademark-level audio choice worth copying. *Estimate: NLC count-ups are comparatively fast (~2–4s to resolve even large wins) with tiered threshold stings rather than long coin-rain loops; skip-on-click is always available.*
- **Max win is a ceremony and a brand.** Hitting max win ends the round with a dedicated MAX WIN splash (game-themed art, e.g. Folsom Prison's 'Meet Your Maker' sequence at 75,000x). Off-game, Nolimit runs **Max Win Legend**: the first 5 players to hit max win on each new game get a limited-edition framed poster (1-of-5, submit Round ID + replay link), plus a public wall-of-fame showing how many posters remain per title, and a Streamer Legends page. The max win is a named, scarce, collectible destination — this converts extreme math into community mythology.
- **Record culture:** near-max wins become news (Bigwinboard: "Tombstone RIP Sets New Record With 271,754x Win!"), and NLC feeds it with replay links and published odds.

## Audio identity: diegetic grit, built by one guy in Ableton

NLC's long-time in-house sound designer **David** (5+ years, guitarist, still uses his Roland MC-505 from age 17) works in **Ableton Live** with KOMPLETE/VST libraries plus real guitar, bass and drums, and does his own mixing/mastering, testing across multiple speakers and headphones.

**Concrete techniques from his Bigwinboard interview:**
- **Reverb on "99% of all sound effects"** — used to place every SFX inside the game's physical environment (mine shaft, cell block, asylum corridor). This is the single most actionable NLC audio rule: SFX share the room of the art.
- Process: sees the game art first, "imagines how that environment sounds," writes demo music, then iterates with the game team.
- Constraint-driven: file size is the top challenge; "the most challenging thing is to create short music loops that will not be boring after a while." Games ship multiple track variations per bonus level (normal, super, etc.).
- No copyrighted samples ever; for East Coast vs West Coast he performed the rap vocals himself; Punk Rocker got vocals specifically "to prevent monotony" in a fast loop.
- Music stays rhythmically simple by design — "soundtracks that run smoothly without complicated rhythms that distract players."

**Theme-diegetic palette (from reviews):** Tombstone: plucked banjos, gunshot stings that "nail down the thematic coffin lid," coin clinks; Tombstone RIP darkens it to "a grim, ambient soundtrack layered with eerie creaks, gunshots, and the distant sound of a hanging." Mental: "slithering soundtrack," flickering-light buzzes, sepia silence. Fire in the Hole: "heavy plodding" mine soundtrack; dynamite crackle. Folsom Prison: bluesy rock shifting to punk in free spins (tempo/energy layer swap on bonus entry). San Quentin: alarms and prison foley.

**Event-audio character — analyst estimates (not published, from observation):** reel stops are heavy, damped, low-mid mechanical thuds (~80–200Hz body, short tail) rather than bright casino 'ticks' — they read as machinery in the themed space. xNudge sequences carry a per-step percussive clunk with rising pitch/tension per step plus multiplier tick; scatter teases slow the remaining reels and add a riser + theme sting (standard for the genre, NLC's are harsher/noisier than NetEnt-style shimmer). xBomb: fuse sizzle pre-roll ~200–400ms before detonation so the boom lands on the visual frame. Win stings are short, dry-ish, and theme-instrumented (banjo/guitar/brass hit) rather than generic major-key arpeggios; celebration audio escalates by tier and — in horror titles — deliberately withholds (Mental's silent count-up ending).

## Volatility communication: honesty as branding

NLC doesn't hide the brutality — they market it, and the UI does the talking:
- **In-game stats pages publish real odds**, unusual in the industry: hit frequency (e.g. Mental 34.19%, Seamen 20.2%), bonus frequency as "1 in N spins" (Mental 1 in 216, San Quentin 1 in 230, Fire in the Hole 1 in 200, Disorder 1 in 270), 100x+ win odds (Supersized: 1 in 1,156), and even **max-win probability** (Mental: ~1 in 18M spins; Supersized top prize ~1 in 520,000). Every game page headlines RTP + "xBet MAX WIN" (e.g. "96.08% RTP, 300000 xBet MAX WIN").
- **A 1–10 volatility scale they intentionally break**: 10/10 "Extreme" is standard; Tombstone RIP is branded "Insane"; in Jan 2022 they introduced a rating of **12** as a marketing device. The volatility label is part of the game's identity, shown in lobby and info.
- **Nolimit Cap** (Kenneth Must Die): player-selectable max win — 69,000x default vs 10,000x with more frequent feature triggers ("lower volatility (still extreme)") — volatility as a player-facing dial.
- **xRIP** (Tombstone RIP) voids any total spin win below stake — a mechanic that exists purely to steepen the distribution, and it's explained plainly in the rules.
- **Multiple RTP builds**: ~96% default, ~94%, ~92/90% operator-selectable (Fire in the Hole ships 96.06 / 94.11 / 90.02). Widely criticized (most casinos run ~94%), but the versions are disclosed per game in the paytable. Feature buys are tiered and priced transparently (Mental: 80x / 200x / 1,000x / 220x lucky-dip; San Quentin 100x–2,000x), with RTP per buy printed (Mental buys raise RTP to 96.47%).
- **Art/audio signal it too:** the harsh minimal base-game audio, dry spells, locked/greyed grid positions (Folsom starts at 16 ways with most of the board visibly caged) all communicate "this game owes you nothing until it explodes."

## Pacing: fast base loop, engineered droughts

- **Base spins are snappy.** *Estimate: full spin cycle ~1.2–1.6s at normal speed, sub-1s feel with quick-stop; reel stops are staggered tightly with no dead air between spin end and next spin availability.* Dead spins resolve with near-zero ceremony — critical, because the math produces LOTS of them (or, in cascade titles like FitH, a ~1-in-4 hit rate of tiny wins that keep the loop ticking).
- **Action Spins (Dec 2024/2025 rollout)** — beyond turbo: "eliminates game animations entirely… powers through multiple rounds per second," available in base AND bonus modes via the Bonus Buy menu, retrofitted to Mental, Fire in the Hole, San Quentin, Tombstone (RIP), Deadwood, The Crypt, xWays Hoarder and more. NLC explicitly builds for players who treat the base game as a grind toward the bonus.
- **Anticipation is spent only where it pays:** scatter teases, xNudge step-throughs, Enhancer Cell openings, Wild Mining conversions. Everything else is fast. The cadence rule: dead spin = instant, near-event = milked (*estimate: a 2-scatter tease adds ~1.5–3s of slowed reel + riser*), feature event = fully choreographed.
- **Bonus pacing inverts the base:** free spins are slow, loaded, every spin carries persistent state (sticky multipliers that never reset in Hang 'em High, jumping wilds relocating, sticky Fire Frames), so each spin is watched, not ground through.

## Why grinders love Nolimit — the formula, and what to copy in PixiJS

**The synthesis:** (1) One visible number to worship — every mechanic feeds an on-screen multiplier that ratchets upward in discrete, audible steps; (2) transform-then-pay sequencing — the board mutates (nudge/split/bomb/unlock) BEFORE evaluation, so hope is manufactured mid-spin; (3) honest brutality — published odds, 10/10+ labels, xRIP: grinders respect a game that states its terms; (4) named scarcity — max win is a marketed, collectible event (Max Win Legend posters, record news cycle); (5) streamer-native math — 500-spin droughts are content, 12,415x bonuses are clips; (6) a fast, frictionless grind loop (Action Spins) between those clips; (7) total aesthetic commitment — diegetic audio (reverb-matched to the environment, real instruments, harsh minimal cues) and dark art nobody else ships.

**Actionable for a PixiJS studio chasing 'das echte Slot-Gefühl':**
1. Make every feature increment ONE persistent multiplier badge, with a per-step transient sound synced to the animation frame (nudge-thunk pattern: step anim + bass thud + number punch, ~150–250ms/step — estimate).
2. Sequence grid transformations strictly BEFORE win evaluation and pay them out sequentially, never simultaneously.
3. Put reverb on effectively all SFX, tuned to the theme's physical space; keep music loops rhythmically simple, ship an intensity variant per bonus tier, swap on bonus entry (Folsom's rock→punk shift).
4. Convert near-misses into events (Wild Mining: dead horizontal 3+ lines become wilds + board blast).
5. Kill all ceremony on dead spins; spend anticipation only on teases and feature steps; offer an animation-skip/ultra mode (Action Spins pattern).
6. Publish the stats in the info screen: hit rate, "bonus 1 in N," max-win odds, RTP per buy — it reads as confidence.
7. Give the max win a name, a screen, and a legend system.
8. Dare one 'signature subversion' per game (Mental's count-up ending in silence) — that's what reviewers remember.
